When I knew It was time to leave for Thailand.

Standing outside a bar in Lower Manhattan, my fists tight, waiting for the guy I called out to fight to face me. Black, like I was, and just as tall, it would had been a fair fight. I waited, but the only person who came out was his friend and co-worker, who was White. Why do the races matter? Because the White guy called me "nigga" and what was a goodnight went downhill pretty quick.


Their names escape me, but for now let's call the White guy Paul, since he looked just like Paul Wall. Paul actually meant it with love, but I wasn't having it, for so many reasons, and when I told him that, Paul apologized. We were cool, though his Black friend wasn't having it. Again, despite how long we talked and drank before things got tense, I forgot the Black dude's name, so we'll call him Ham, since he's an idiot, and in my recollection, looks like a 220 pound bag of hamburger helper.


"You're just gonna disrespect my boy after all the love he showed you?" Ham had said, getting more worked up as he went on. Shocked, I left the bar, thinking about just how ridiculous things had gotten, and sadly, I was gonna make it worse. Staying away would had been the smart thing, but that option left a while ago; in the arms race of doing dumb shit, Ham was gonna be Iraq, and I was gonna be the U.S.


The irony of the fact that I was gonna maul another Black person in the the highly gentrified neighborhood because I refused to let his White friend call me a nigga,my nigga, a nigger or any varition of word, is not lost on me, but I was beyond mad, beyond even reason....at least for a while. For good while, Ham never stepped outside the bar while Paul tried to talk me out of it. Eventually, I walked away, again, and for good. This was just one more thing, on top of so many things; from the subtle to no to subtle racism of Manhattan that my friends, family, and I deal with, failing school, my alcoholic father, and my dangerously following his footsteps, I knew I had to change. Something had to change, I had to change, and to do that, I had to go.
